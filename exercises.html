Workouts

Choose a level that is right for you. If workouts take longer than 30 minutes or less than 5 minutes, decrease or increase your level respectively. Warm up slowly, use full range of motion and good form, and stop if something hurts.

Safety: Consult your physician before starting a fitness program. The only equipment you may need for the core program is a pull-up bar. Pull-up substitutions are fine.

Exercise explanations

Pull-up

Grasp a bar and pull until your chin clears it, then lower until arms and shoulders are fully extended. Use the grip that feels right and alternate grips.

Push-up

Raise and lower your body with your arms while keeping your back flat. Try to keep elbows close to your sides.

Squat

Bend at knees and hips, lower the torso between the legs, then stand. Keep torso upright, weight on heels, and knees tracking safely.

Pull-up substitutions

If you cannot do a pull-up yet, use substitutions. A rough rule from the legacy content: 3 substitutions = 1 regular pull-up.

  • Body rows using a towel or rope over a bar.
  • Assisted pull-ups with feet on a box.
  • Jumping pull-ups.
  • Chair-assisted hold at the top.
  • Slow negatives from the top position.
